Ingredients

  • Chopped bitter leaf - about 400g
  • 400g beef
  • Moru fumé (smoked fish)
  • Groundnut - about 600ml boiled and blended
  • 5 tbsps ground crayfish
  • 3 scotch bonnet chillies (ghost pepper)
  • 2 medium onions
  • 2 inches of ginger
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 3 Maggi Crevête cubes
  •  
  •  

Tips

Regular Maggi cubes or any crayfish stock cubes can be used instead of Maggi Crevête cubes

Add king prawns to make this dish even more interesting

Method

  1. Blend 1/2 onion, ginger, garlic and 1 scotch bonnet.
  2. Bring about 600ml of salted water to the boil.
  3. Add blended spices, beef, and 1 Maggi Crevête cube. Cook until tender.
  4. Remove the meat from the pot but leave the stock in. Clean the fish properly and cook it in the meat stock for 10 minutes.
  5. Finely chop the remaining 1 1/2 onion and fry in some vegetable oil.
  6. Add groundnut once the onions have softened and bring to the boil.
  7. Now add meat stock and cook on high heat while stirring occasionally. Do this until the mixture starts thickening and sticking to the bottom of the pot.
  8. Turn the heat to absolute minimum then add beef,fish, bitter leaf, crayfish, 1 cube and 1 chilli. Taste to see if the extra chilli and cube are required. Add them if necessary.
  9. Allow to simmer on medium heat for at least 15 minutes.
  10. You know it is ready when the water has dried up and oil starts rising to the surface.
