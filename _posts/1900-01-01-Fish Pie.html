Ingredients

  • 225g self-raising flour
  • 110g butter (softened at room temp.)
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 3-4 tbsp cold water
  • 3x pinch of salt
  • 3 cans of sardines (mashed)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 3 sticks of spring onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 scotch bonnet chillies (finely chopped)
  • Mixed vegetables (optional)
  •  

Tips

Replace sardines with minced beef to make ‘Pili Pili’

Glaze the pies with egg before putting in the oven for an extra golden colour

Tuna can be used to replace sardines as a less oily alternative

Method

  1. Sieve flour, salt & baking powder into a bowl
  2. Slice butter into cubes and mix with the dry ingredients until a crumbly mixture is formed
  3. Beat the egg and add to the crumbly mixture
  4. Mix thoroughly and add water 1 tbsp at a time until the dough can be rolled into a ball
  5. Wrap the dough ball in cling film and refrigerate for 10 minutes
  6. While the dough is in the fridge, stir fry the vegetables for about 3 minutes
  7. Add the sardines to the frying pan and stir fry for 5 more minutes
  8. Take the dough out of the fridge. Knead it and roll it out as thinly as possible
  9. Cut it out into about 20-30 circles and line on a baking tray
  10. Put some filling into each circle and seal the edges using a fork
  11. Pre-heat oven to 200°C and bake pies for 4 minutes on each side
