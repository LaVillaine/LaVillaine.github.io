Ingredients

  • 4 green plantains
  • 500g oxtail
  • 200g diced beef
  • 250ml palm oil
  • Mbonga - Dry fish [optional]
  • Scotch bonnet chillies (ghost pepper)
  • 1 chopped onion
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 inches of ginger
  • 2 tbsp ground njangsa
  • 1/2 stem of leek
  • 1 tbsp country spices [optional]
  •  

Tips

Country spices used are: Pebe[Monodora Myristica], Country Onion[Afrostyrax Lepidophyllus], Essesse[Tetrapleura Tetraptera], Njangsa[Ricinodendron Heudelotii], Bongo [Aframomum Melegueta]

 

Method

  1. Peel and wash the plantains. Cut into 6 pieces each and keep in a bowl of water.
  2. Blend all the spices together: onion, pepper, garlic, ginger, leek, njangsa, country spices.

     

     

  3. Heat the oil in a large pot. Salt the meat and stir fry for 5 minutes on medium heat.
  4. Add plantains, half the blended spices, salt, 2 maggi cubes and half a litre of water.

     

     

  5. Bring the mixture to the boil and cook on high heat for 20 minutes.
  6. Add the remaining spices and mbonga. Cook for another 30 minutes on medium heat while stirring occasionally.

     

  7. Stab the plantain and meat with a fork to make sure they are tender. If not, cook for another 5 - 10 minutes.
